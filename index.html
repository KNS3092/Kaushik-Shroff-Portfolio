About Me

I’m a graduate student in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Wisconsin–Madison with a strong interest in computer architecture, ASIC design, and design for testability (DFT). I enjoy working across different layers of digital systems—from RTL design and simulation to architectural modeling and test methodology. My projects have given me hands-on experience with processor internals, custom test generation, hardware accelerators, and performance/power analysis. I’m especially motivated by opportunities to improve efficiency, scalability, and reliability in complex hardware systems. I enjoy learning new tools, solving practical engineering problems, and collaborating on ideas that bridge theory and implementation. Outside of academics, I like building PCs, exploring new hardware technologies, and keeping up with the latest in tech.
